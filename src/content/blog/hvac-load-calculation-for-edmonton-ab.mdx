# HVAC Load Calculation Guide for Edmonton, AB

Determining the correct HVAC size for a home in **Edmonton, AB** requires precise engineering data. Edmonton's unique climate requires careful consideration of both peak heating and cooling loads.

## Local Climate Design Data

In **Edmonton**, HVAC systems must be designed to handle the extremes of both winter heating and summer cooling. According to engineering databases:

*   **Winter Design Temperature (99%):** -22°F
*   **Summer Design Temperature (1%):** 80°F
*   **Latent Grains Difference:** 15 gr/lb

For a system to maintain a comfortable 70°F indoors during a winter freeze, it must overcome a temperature difference (ΔT) of **92°F**.

## Estimated Operating Costs in Edmonton

With an average electricity rate of **$0.18/kWh** in AB, system efficiency is critical. For instance, a typical 3-ton system in Edmonton might cost approximately **$570** per month to operate during peak summer months. 

Over-sizing your equipment (a common mistake in AB) doesn't just increase your installation cost; it leads to short-cycling, which can spike your utility bills by 15-20% due to inefficient startup draws.

## Why Precision Sizing Matters in AB

In AB's unique climate, an "oversized" system is just as problematic as an undersized one. Edmonton homes often suffer from:

1.  **Poor Dehumidification:** The AC doesn't run long enough to pull moisture from the air.
2.  **Shortened Lifespan:** Increased wear and tear on the compressor.
3.  **High Utility Bills:** Starting a motor consumes more energy than letting it run steadily.
