# HVAC Load Calculation Guide for Ottawa, ON

Determining the correct HVAC size for a home in **Ottawa, ON** requires precise engineering data. Ottawa's unique climate requires careful consideration of both peak heating and cooling loads.

## Local Climate Design Data

In **Ottawa**, HVAC systems must be designed to handle the extremes of both winter heating and summer cooling. According to engineering databases:

*   **Winter Design Temperature (99%):** -4°F
*   **Summer Design Temperature (1%):** 86°F
*   **Latent Grains Difference:** 35 gr/lb

For a system to maintain a comfortable 70°F indoors during a winter freeze, it must overcome a temperature difference (ΔT) of **74°F**.

With an average electricity rate of **$0.14/kWh** in ON, system efficiency is critical. For instance, a typical 3-ton system in Ottawa might cost approximately **$443** per month to operate during peak summer months. 

Over-sizing your equipment (a common mistake in ON) doesn't just increase your installation cost; it leads to short-cycling, which can spike your utility bills by 15-20% due to inefficient startup draws.

## Why Precision Sizing Matters in ON

In ON's unique climate, an "oversized" system is just as problematic as an undersized one. Ottawa homes often suffer from:

1.  **Poor Dehumidification:** The AC doesn't run long enough to pull moisture from the air.
2.  **Shortened Lifespan:** Increased wear and tear on the compressor.
3.  **High Utility Bills:** Starting a motor consumes more energy than letting it run steadily.
