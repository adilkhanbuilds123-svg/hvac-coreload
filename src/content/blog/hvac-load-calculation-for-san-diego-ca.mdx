# HVAC Load Calculation Guide for San Diego, CA

Determining the correct HVAC size for a home in **San Diego, CA** requires precise engineering data. Coastal moisture vs. inland heat creates unique load profiles that simple square-footage rules miss.

## Local Climate Design Data

In **San Diego**, HVAC systems must be designed to handle the extremes of both winter heating and summer cooling. According to engineering databases:

*   **Winter Design Temperature (99%):** 44°F
*   **Summer Design Temperature (1%):** 80°F
*   **Latent Grains Difference:** 15 gr/lb

For a system to maintain a comfortable 70°F indoors during a winter freeze, it must overcome a temperature difference (ΔT) of **26°F**.

With an average electricity rate of **$0.32/kWh** in CA, system efficiency is critical. For instance, a typical 3-ton system in San Diego might cost approximately **$1013** per month to operate during peak summer months. 

Over-sizing your equipment (a common mistake in CA) doesn't just increase your installation cost; it leads to short-cycling, which can spike your utility bills by 15-20% due to inefficient startup draws.

## Why Precision Sizing Matters in CA

In CA's unique climate, an "oversized" system is just as problematic as an undersized one. San Diego homes often suffer from:

1.  **Poor Dehumidification:** The AC doesn't run long enough to pull moisture from the air.
2.  **Shortened Lifespan:** Increased wear and tear on the compressor.
3.  **High Utility Bills:** Starting a motor consumes more energy than letting it run steadily.
