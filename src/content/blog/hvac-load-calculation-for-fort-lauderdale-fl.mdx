# HVAC Load Calculation Guide for Fort Lauderdale, FL

Determining the correct HVAC size for a home in **Fort Lauderdale, FL** requires precise engineering data. With high humidity levels and tropical summer days, dehumidification is just as important as cooling.

## Local Climate Design Data

In **Fort Lauderdale**, HVAC systems must be designed to handle the extremes of both winter heating and summer cooling. According to engineering databases:

*   **Winter Design Temperature (99%):** 50°F
*   **Summer Design Temperature (1%):** 90°F
*   **Latent Grains Difference:** 65 gr/lb

For a system to maintain a comfortable 70°F indoors during a winter freeze, it must overcome a temperature difference (ΔT) of **20°F**.

Over-sizing your equipment (a common mistake in FL) doesn't just increase your installation cost; it leads to short-cycling, which can spike your utility bills by 15-20% due to inefficient startup draws.

## Why Precision Sizing Matters in FL

In FL's unique climate, an "oversized" system is just as problematic as an undersized one. Fort Lauderdale homes often suffer from:

1.  **Poor Dehumidification:** The AC doesn't run long enough to pull moisture from the air.
2.  **Shortened Lifespan:** Increased wear and tear on the compressor.
3.  **High Utility Bills:** Starting a motor consumes more energy than letting it run steadily.
