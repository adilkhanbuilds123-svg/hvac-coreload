# HVAC Load Calculation Guide for Portland, OR

Determining the correct HVAC size for a home in **Portland, OR** requires precise engineering data. Portland's unique climate requires careful consideration of both peak heating and cooling loads.

## Local Climate Design Data

In **Portland**, HVAC systems must be designed to handle the extremes of both winter heating and summer cooling. According to engineering databases:

*   **Winter Design Temperature (99%):** 25°F

## Why Precision Sizing Matters in OR

In OR's unique climate, an "oversized" system is just as problematic as an undersized one. Portland homes often suffer from:

1.  **Poor Dehumidification:** The AC doesn't run long enough to pull moisture from the air.
2.  **Shortened Lifespan:** Increased wear and tear on the compressor.
3.  **High Utility Bills:** Starting a motor consumes more energy than letting it run steadily.
